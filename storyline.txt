The *Ramayana* narrates the story of Rama, the crown prince of Ayodhya and an avatar (incarnation) of the Hindu god Vishnu. Here's a brief overview:

1. **Rama's Birth and Early Life**:
   - Rama was born in the kingdom of Ayodhya (also known as Oudh) to King Dasharatha and Queen Kausalya.
   - His tutelage began under the sage Vishvamitra, who taught him various skills and weapons.

2. **The Bridegroom Tournament and Sita's Swayamvara**:
   - To win the hand of Sita, the daughter of King Janaka, Rama participated in a bridegroom tournament.
   - He successfully bent Lord Shiva's mighty bow, winning Sita as his wife.

3. **Exile to the Forest**:
   - Due to palace intrigue, Rama was unjustly banished from his position as heir to the kingdom.
   - Accompanied by his wife Sita and his half-brother Lakshmana, Rama spent 14 years in exile in the forest.

4. **The Abduction of Sita**:
   - While in the forest, the demon-king Ravana abducted Sita.
   - Rama and Lakshmana, busy pursuing a golden deer sent to mislead them, were unable to prevent it.

5. **Alliance with Sugriva and Hanuman**:
   - Rama formed an alliance with Sugriva, the monkey king, and his general Hanuman.
   - Together, they planned to rescue Sita from Lanka.

6. **The Battle with Ravana**:
   - Rama and his allies attacked Lanka, Ravana's capital.
   - In a fierce battle, Rama slew Ravana and rescued Sita.

7. **Sita's Ordeal and Reunion**:
   - To prove her chastity after captivity, Sita underwent an ordeal by fire.
   - Upon their return to Ayodhya, Rama faced doubts about Sita's purity and banished her to the forest.
   - There, she gave birth to Rama's twin sons.

8. **Sita's Return to the Earth**:
   - After reuniting with her sons, Sita protested her innocence once more.
   - She then plunged into the earth, where her mother received her and swallowed her up.
